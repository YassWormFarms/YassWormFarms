A Few Interesting Microbe Facts

  • Pseudomonas: Enables the plant to tolerate lower frost temperatures.
  • Sorangium: Produces secondary fungicides and bactericides that reduce competition of pathogens in the soil.
  • Penicillium: Fungi that produce enzymes, antibiotics, and growth regulators.
  • Methylobacterium: Stimulates seed germination.
  • Streptomyces: Provides antifungals, antivirals, and antibiotics.
  • Myxococcus: Efficient predator on unwanted bacteria.
  • Lactobacillus: Helps plants resist heat, radiation, toxic chemicals, and pH extremes.

Red Wigglers (compost worms) are known to produce these types of microbes – we use mostly this variety of compost worm.

Important Storage Information

  • Our Worm Juice Fertilizer is alive and must be stored in the shade, kept cool, and vented.
  • Care must be taken NOT to store in a container that has had anything other than water stored in it.
  • Do not use town water to mix for foliar spraying as the chlorine can kill the microbes.
