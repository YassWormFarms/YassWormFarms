DIY Worm Farm System

Your Own Windrow Worm Farm

Your own windrow worm farm can produce up to 1000 litres every fortnight. The following is a rough outline of costs — for more information click the Contact tab.

  • Need slope minimum 3.5 to 4 Degrees
  • Access to non-town water (no chlorine)
  • Front end loader (or ability to hire/borrow one)
  • 7 IBCs
  • Roll of high impact plastic or cement pad
  • Rhino board if not using concrete
  • High impact tap
  • Drip lines
  • Collection pipe and poly
  • Small petrol pump
  • Compressor and pump for fermentation tank ~$500
  • Truck load stable waste (50 ton) or similar to create base windrows — needs to be 60:40 carbon to food (not chicken waste)
  • Truckload (10 cubic m) of worm windrow containing millions of worms from Yass Worm Farms
  • 55 micron filter
  • Various fittings
  • Consulting fees — see below for more details
  • Freight
  • Humic Acid

Estimated Costs: $7,000 to $10,000 depending on location. I would supply all the information you need — access to supplies and full continuing instructions — but you would need to do the setup work yourself. — James Brown
