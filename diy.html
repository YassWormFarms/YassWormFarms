DIY Worm Farm System

Build Your Own Worm Farm

Follow our step-by-step guide to create a DIY worm farm that reduces fertiliser costs and improves soil health. Worm juice from your farm can supplement or replace commercial fertilisers, unlocking nutrients naturally present in your soil.

Why DIY Worm Farms Work

  • Low-cost alternative to chemical fertilisers.
  • Produces nutrient-rich worm juice for your plants.
  • Enhances soil biology for long-term fertility.

Estimated Savings

Using worm juice in combination with reduced superphosphate application can save $30–$100 per hectare per season. You can also use our home page calculator to estimate your savings based on your farm size.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Select a shaded, ventilated location.
2. Prepare worm bins using pallets or containers.
3. Add bedding (shredded paper, cardboard, straw).
4. Introduce compost worms (red wigglers recommended).
5. Feed organic kitchen scraps and maintain moisture.
6. Harvest worm juice and castings regularly.

DIY Budget Tip

Building your own worm farm can save hundreds of dollars compared to buying commercial setups. With just a few materials and some time, you can produce nutrient-rich worm juice to reduce fertiliser costs.
