Rough Estimated Cost of Setting Up Your Own Worm Juice Production

  • Need slope minimum 3.5 to 4 Degrees.
  • Access to non-town water (no chlorine)
  • Front end loader (or ability to hire one)
  • 7 IBC's x $150 each
  • Roll of high impact plastic $140
  • Rhino board $1500
  • High impact tape $100
  • Drip lines $100
  • Collection pipe and poly $400
  • Petrol pump $150
  • Compressor and pump for fermentation tank $500
  • Truckload (10 cubic) of worm windrow containing millions of worms from Yass Worm Farms $3500
  • Coffee grinds collection containers $100
  • 55 micron filter $250
  • Various fittings $200
  • Freight $2000
  • Humic Acid $80

Total Average Cost: $10,000 to $12,000

You will need a supply of stable waste or something similar to set up your initial windrow. It could be any manure plus straw (not chicken). Racecourses have stable waste, saleyards have manure, and piggeries are a great source of worm food. You need approximately 60–70% carbon and 30–40% food such as manure.
